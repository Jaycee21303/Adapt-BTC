Lightning Deep Dive

Lightning payments rely on smart contracts called HTLCs and active liquidity management.

HTLCs

Hashed Time-Locked Contracts enforce conditional payments; either the receiver reveals a preimage or funds revert after timeout.

Liquidity

Outbound liquidity allows sending; inbound liquidity allows receiving. Balancing channels is critical.

Routing nodes

Well-connected nodes provide payment paths and earn routing fees.

LN invoices

Invoices encode amount, memo, expiry, and payment hash. They guide the network to the receiver.

Multi-path

Multi-path payments split a payment across several routes to improve reliability.

Summary

  • HTLCs make Lightning trust-minimized.
  • Liquidity determines whether payments can send or receive.
  • Routing nodes and MPP improve reliability.
