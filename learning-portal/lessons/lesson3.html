Mining

Mining secures Bitcoin by turning energy into economic weight behind every block.

Mining process

Miners gather transactions, build a candidate block, and repeatedly hash the block header until the hash meets the target.

Difficulty

The network adjusts difficulty roughly every two weeks to keep block times near 10 minutes.

ASICs

Specialized hardware (ASICs) dominate mining because they hash efficiently and competitively.

Halving

Block rewards cut in half every 210,000 blocks, reducing new supply and reinforcing scarcity.

Energy usage

Energy expenditure anchors Bitcoin’s security; miners seek low-cost, often stranded or renewable power.

Hashrate

Network hashrate measures total computational effort. Higher hashrate increases attack costs.

Summary

  • Proof-of-work mining converts energy into security.
  • Difficulty retargets keep issuance predictable.
  • Halvings enforce declining new supply.
