What Is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is the first widely adopted form of digital property—scarce, portable, and secured by an open network.

Digital property

Bitcoin exists as entries in a public ledger. Ownership is controlled by private keys rather than accounts at intermediaries.

Scarcity (21M)

The protocol caps supply at 21 million coins, released on a predictable schedule. This hard cap creates digital scarcity.

Decentralization

No single entity controls Bitcoin. Thousands of nodes validate rules, miners add blocks, and anyone can self-verify.

Why Bitcoin was created

Satoshi launched Bitcoin in 2009 as a response to fragile banking and to enable peer-to-peer money without trusted third parties.

Importance of sats

Each bitcoin splits into 100,000,000 satoshis (sats). Quoting values in sats makes small payments intuitive.

Bitcoin vs fiat

Fiat currencies inflate as supply expands. Bitcoin’s fixed supply and transparent issuance make it resistant to debasement.

Bitcoin vs gold

Gold is scarce but heavy to move and hard to audit. Bitcoin moves at internet speed and is auditable by anyone.

Global network

Bitcoin operates 24/7 with no borders. Anyone with internet access can send, receive, and validate transactions.

Summary

  • Bitcoin is digital property with a fixed 21M supply.
  • Decentralized validation keeps it neutral and censorship-resistant.
  • Sats enable small, global payments.
